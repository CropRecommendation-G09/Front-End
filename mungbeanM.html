Preparation before sowing

Soil preparation for growing mung beans includes choosing a well-drained, loose soil rich in organic matter with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5, and ensuring it provides plenty of sunlight and appropriate moisture.

Sowing method

The method of sowing mung beans is to spread the seeds evenly on the pre-prepared soil, cover with a thin layer of soil, keep the soil moist but avoid overwatering, provide sufficient sunlight and suitable temperature, wait for germination and carry out timely thinning and management.

Best time to sow

The best time to sow mung beans is in warm seasons such as spring or summer when the soil temperature is steadily above 20 degrees Celsius.

Post-sowing care

Mung bean care after planting includes keeping the soil moist but avoiding overwatering, providing adequate sunlight, weeding regularly, fertilizing to promote growth, controlling pests and diseases, and regular observation and adjustments to ensure healthy growth and a good harvest.

Harvest and Next Steps

The harvest time for mung beans is generally 60 to 90 days after sowing. It is judged based on the observed growth status of the plants and the maturity of the pods. The pods can be harvested when they become plump, turn yellow in color, and the beans inside are full.
