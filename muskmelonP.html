Preparation before sowing

Soil preparation for growing melons involves choosing fertile, loose, well-drained soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5, while adding organic matter and an appropriate amount of fertilizer to provide a nutrient-rich growing environment.

Sowing method

The method of sowing melons is to dig grooves or build ridges in the pre-prepared soil, spread the seeds evenly in the grooves or on the ridges, cover with a thin layer of soil, maintain appropriate moisture and temperature, and carry out timely thinning and adjustment after germination.

Best time to sow

The best time to sow melons is in warm seasons, such as spring or summer, when soil temperatures are stable above 18 degrees Celsius to ensure that the seeds can germinate and grow quickly.

Post-sowing care

Melon care after planting includes keeping the soil moist but avoiding overwatering, providing adequate sunlight, weeding regularly, fertilizing to promote growth, supporting spreading plants, adjusting temperatures, controlling pests and diseases, and regularly observing and taking necessary measures to ensure healthy growth and a good harvest.

Harvest and Next Steps

The harvest time of melons depends on the variety and climatic conditions. Generally, it is harvested about 70 to 90 days after sowing, judging by the observed maturity indicators of the fruit appearance, when the skin changes color, the fruit becomes plump, the sound is dull and the fruit stem is easy to separate.
