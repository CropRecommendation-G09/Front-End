Preparation before sowing

Soil preparation for growing rice involves selecting soil that is rich in organic matter, well-drained, with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0, while ensuring that the soil is rich in nutrients, has a fine-grained soil texture, and practices proper rice field land preparation and water management to provide suitable growing conditions.

Sowing method

Rice is typically grown from seeds that are soaked overnight and then broadcasted or sown directly into flooded fields.

Best time to sow

Rice is usually sown in the spring after the last frost has passed and when temperatures are consistently warm.

Post-sowing care

Maintain flooded or submerged conditions in the rice fields throughout the growing season. Keep weeds under control and monitor for pests and diseases.

Harvest and Next Steps

Rice is typically ready for harvest in the fall. Harvest when the grains are mature and the plants have turned golden brown. Once harvested, rice can be dried and stored for later use.
