Preparation before sowing

Soil preparation for growing papayas involves choosing a soil that is rich in organic matter, well-drained, and has a pH between 5.5 and 7.0, while ensuring that the soil is rich in nutrients and has microbial activity to provide a suitable growing environment.

Sowing method

Papaya seeds should be planted directly into the soil, about 1 inch deep. They can also be started in pots or trays and transplanted later.

Best time to sow

Papaya seeds can be sown year-round in warm climates. In cooler climates, it's best to sow seeds in the spring.

Post-sowing care

Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Provide partial shade for young plants. Fertilize regularly with a balanced fertilizer.

Harvest and Next Steps

Papayas typically mature in about 6-9 months after planting. Harvest when the fruit is mostly yellow and yield to gentle pressure. Once harvested, papayas can be eaten fresh or used in various dishes.
