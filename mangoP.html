Preparation before sowing

The soil should be selected to have good aeration and water retention and should be rich in nutrients to support its growth and fruit development. Sufficient root space is also required. The soil pH for suitable growth should be between 6.0 and 7.5.

Sowing method

The sowing method of mango includes selecting mature seeds, burying them in suitable soil, keeping them moist and in a warm environment, and transplanting them after germination and providing proper care.

Best time to sow

The best time to sow mangoes is during a season with warm temperatures and plenty of sunshine to promote seed germination and healthy growth of seedlings.

Post-sowing care

Mango seedling care after sowing includes timely watering, providing sufficient sunlight, controlling temperature, fertilizing, weeding, preventing and controlling pests and diseases, supporting pruning, and regular observation and adjustment.

Harvest and Next Steps

The best time to harvest mangoes is usually when the fruit changes color, the mango aroma is strong, and the flesh has a certain elasticity.
