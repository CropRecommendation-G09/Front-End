Preparation before sowing

Select a shaded area as coffee plants require protection from direct sunlight. Ideal soil is rich, volcanic and well-draining with a pH of 6.0-6.5.Prepare the soil by adding compost and manure to enhance fertility and drainage.

Sowing method

Coffee is typically propagated from seeds or seedlings. Plant seeds or seedlings in prepared holes.

Best time to sow

In tropical climates, coffee can be planted at any time of year, though the start of the rainy season is ideal.

Post-sowing care

Water regularly to keep the soil moist but not waterlogged. Shade young plants from direct sunlight and apply mulch to retain soil moisture.

Harvest and Next Steps

Coffee trees start bearing fruit after 3-4 years. Harvest the cherries when they are bright red and fully ripe. Post-harvest, the beans are extracted, fermented, dried, and roasted based on desired coffee production.
