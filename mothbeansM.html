Preparation before sowing

Soil preparation for growing moth beans includes choosing a loose soil rich in organic matter, ensuring good drainage and proper pH adjustment, so that the pH range is generally between 6.0 and 7.5 to provide a good growing environment.

Sowing method

The method of sowing moth beans is to evenly spread the seeds on the prepared soil, cover with a thin layer of soil, keep it properly moist and provide a suitable temperature, and carry out timely thinning and adjustment after germination.

Best time to sow

The best time to sow moth beans is in warm seasons, such as spring or summer, when the soil temperature is stable above 15 degrees Celsius.

Post-sowing care

The care of moth beans after sowing includes keeping the soil moist but avoiding overwatering, providing sufficient sunlight, regular weeding, timely fertilization to promote growth and control pests and diseases, while monitoring the health of the seedlings and taking necessary measures in time.

Harvest and Next Steps

The harvest time is generally about 60 to 70 days after sowing. It can be harvested when the pods become full and darker in color, judging by the maturity and size of the observed pods.
